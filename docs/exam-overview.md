# CISSP Exam Overview

## About the Exam

The **Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP)** is administered by ISC2 and is the world's most recognised information security certification. It validates deep technical and managerial knowledge across the full spectrum of cybersecurity.

**Official page:** https://www.isc2.org/certifications/cissp

---

## Exam Specifications

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Format | Computerised Adaptive Testing (CAT) |
| Questions | 100–150 items |
| Item Types | Multiple choice + advanced item types (drag-drop, hotspot) |
| Duration | 3 hours |
| Passing Score | 700 out of 1000 (scaled score) |
| Languages | English, Chinese (select windows), German, Japanese, Spanish |
| Testing Centre | Pearson VUE authorised centres |
| Cost | USD $749 (verify current pricing at isc2.org) |

## Understanding CAT (Computerised Adaptive Testing)

This is critical to understand before you sit the exam.

### How it works
- The exam starts with a medium-difficulty question
- If you answer correctly, the next question is harder
- If you answer incorrectly, the next question is easier
- The exam continues until the system reaches **statistical confidence** that you are above or below the passing standard
- The exam ends at any point between 100 and 150 questions

### What this means for you
- **You cannot go back** to review or change previous answers
- Harder questions are a **good sign** — it means the system thinks you might pass
- Easier questions mean the system has concerns — stay focused
- The exam may end at 100 questions — that does not mean you failed
- Answering 150 questions does not mean you passed or failed

### The scoring model
- 700/1000 is a **scaled score**, not a raw percentage
- You are not graded on how many you get right — you are graded on the **difficulty** of what you answered correctly
- This is why CISSP-trained test-takers emphasise mastery, not memorisation

## Experience Requirements

To earn the CISSP (not just pass the exam), you need:

- **Minimum 5 years** of cumulative, paid, full-time work experience
- In **2 or more** of the 8 CISSP domains
- A 4-year university degree (or approved equivalent) waives **1 year** of experience

### Associate of ISC2
If you pass the exam but don't yet have 5 years experience, you become an **Associate of ISC2** and have 6 years to earn the required experience. This is a legitimate path.

---

## After Passing

1. **Endorsement:** An ISC2 member in good standing must endorse you within 9 months of passing
2. **Background check:** ISC2 conducts a review
3. **AMF:** Pay the Annual Maintenance Fee (USD $125/year as of 2024)
4. **CPEs:** Earn 120 Continuing Professional Education credits every 3-year cycle (40/year minimum)

---

## Exam Day Tips

- Arrive 30 minutes early to the Pearson VUE centre
- Bring two forms of valid ID (one must be government-issued with photo and signature)
- No materials allowed in the testing room
- Read every question twice — CISSP questions are deliberately ambiguous
- Eliminate wrong answers first, then choose the BEST answer (not just a correct one)
- Think like a **CISO**, not a sysadmin
