# Full Mock Exam: CISSP Simulation Guide

The CISSP uses Computerised Adaptive Testing (CAT) — you'll face 100–150 questions in 3 hours with no ability to go back. This guide tells you how to run your own timed full-length practice sessions to simulate real exam conditions.

**Do this in Week 8 before booking your exam.**

---

## When You're Ready for a Full Mock

You are ready to run a full mock exam when:

- [ ] All 8 domain quizzes completed with 80%+ on each
- [ ] `resources/exam-traps.md` reviewed and understood
- [ ] `resources/mindset-guide.md` read and internalised
- [ ] `artifacts/formula-cheatsheet.md` memorised (can recall without looking)
- [ ] At least 300 practice questions completed across all domains

If you haven't hit these benchmarks, continue domain study first.

## How to Run a Timed Mock Session

### Setup
1. Find 3 hours of uninterrupted time (no phone, no tabs, no pausing)
2. Use a platform that presents 150 questions in sequence — no going back
3. Set a timer for 3 hours
4. Do not look at notes or reference materials

### During the exam
- Aim for ~72 seconds per question on average (3 hours ÷ 150 questions)
- If stuck, eliminate obviously wrong answers and commit — you cannot return
- Apply the **Manager Filter** on every question (see `resources/mindset-guide.md`)
- Watch for keywords: first, best, most, primary, immediate

### After the exam
- Score your results by domain
- For every wrong answer: identify why the correct answer is correct AND why each wrong answer is wrong
- Log your domain scores in the table below

## Exam Day Checklist

**Night before:**
- [ ] Review `resources/exam-traps.md` one final time
- [ ] Review `artifacts/formula-cheatsheet.md` — especially SLE/ALE/ARO
- [ ] Confirm your Pearson VUE appointment and centre address
- [ ] Set two alarms. Get 7–8 hours sleep.

**Morning of:**
- [ ] Light breakfast — something you tolerate well
- [ ] No cramming. Trust your preparation.
- [ ] Arrive at the Pearson VUE centre 30 minutes early
- [ ] Bring two forms of ID (one government-issued with photo and signature)

**At the centre:**
- [ ] You'll be given scratch paper or a whiteboard — use it for formulas immediately
- [ ] Write out SLE = AV × EF, ALE = SLE × ARO, OSI layers before question 1
- [ ] Read each question twice. Identify what's really being asked.
- [ ] Apply the Manager Filter. Govern first. Technical second.
- [ ] Commit and move forward — no going back.

## The CAT Exam in Practice

The real exam may feel different from practice because of adaptive difficulty. Here's what to expect:

**If questions get harder:** Good sign. The system thinks you're performing above the pass line. Stay focused. Don't overthink.

**If questions seem easier:** The system may be establishing your baseline, or it has concerns. Don't panic — stay consistent and apply your method.

**If the exam ends at 100 questions:** This is not bad news. It means the system reached statistical confidence early. You may have passed decisively — or failed decisively. You won't know until results.

**If you reach 150 questions:** You'll know when you're done. Some candidates need all 150 — this is normal and carries no negative signal.

## After the Exam

### If you pass
1. Screenshot or print the confirmation screen
2. Complete the **endorsement process** within 9 months: https://www.isc2.org/certifications/cissp/endorsement
3. Pay your Annual Maintenance Fee (AMF)
4. Begin tracking CPEs immediately — you need 120 over 3 years

### If you don't pass (first time)
- ISC2 provides a diagnostic report by domain
- You must wait 30 days before your second attempt
- Maximum 3 attempts per year
- Use the domain report to target study precisely
- Most candidates who study properly pass on their second attempt

### Endorsement Process
An ISC2 member in good standing must verify your professional experience. If you don't know one:
- Post in the ISC2 Community forums: https://community.isc2.org
- ISC2 itself will endorse you if no member is available (for the Associate path)
